                \item \textbf{t-SNE plot of trained model}\\
    263
                Using t-stochastic neighbour embeddings (t-SNE) algorithm, we can build a 2-dimension representation of our trained high-dimension embeddings. We expect the 'similar' words to exist in similar context and we hope that the 2-dimension representation of our embeddings also shows this syntactic similarity by Euclidean distances in 2-D. After deriving the 2-dimension representations using t-SNE algorithm, a k-mean clustering algorithm was used to automatically classify the embeddings into clusters. We empirically adjusted the total number of clusters to form and eventually form 32 clusters for Gutenberg corpus and 30 clusters for Penn corpus. The results of t-SNE plots and their k-mean clusters are shown below. Observe that both Penn and Gutenberg corpus successfully separate embeddings into cluster while Gutenberg corpus, with a larger corpus size, was able to separate embeddings with a clearer boundary (Figure 2 and 3). There exist several intuitively reasonable clusters in both set of embeddings. For example, the cluster 30 of Gutenberg (Figure 4) seems to ensemble several plural of nouns together. Cluster 10 of Penn (Figure 5) seems to be a set of human names and alias. One can also observe some related words inside each cluster. For example in cluster 30 of Gutenberg, 'cavaliers', 'knights','troopers' and 'regiments' are closed to each other, which seems to tell a war legend.
